ORIGINS OF 3D GRAPHICS

FLEX FRIDAY SESSION

Introduction

    Making a 2D image on a computer is easy enough - open a paint program, draw some pixels, and save it. It's easy to imagine how a game engine moves around these pixels to create 2D games... but how do computers render 3D graphics? How do they take a set of points in 3D space and figure out how to display them on our 2-dimensional screens? Must involve math, right?

    For a long time, I let myself be scared by that prospect and avoided researching further, but over Spring Break, I got curious and borrowed a book from the library that opened my mind - turns out, it's not too difficult, especially for the basics! So that's where we'll start - drawing 2d shapes and rotating them. As a proof of concept, here's a more complex, WIP demo of a 3D engine running in Makecode Arcade (similar to Scratch) that I developed with the help of that book.

What we will do in this session...
